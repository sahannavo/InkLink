Essential Writing Tips

Practical advice to improve your writing skills and engage your readers

Show, Don't Tell

Instead of telling readers how a character feels, show it through their actions, dialogue, and body language.

Instead of: "Sarah was nervous."

Try: "Sarah's hands trembled as she fumbled with the keys, her breath catching in her throat."

  • Use sensory details
  • Reveal emotions through actions
  • Trust your readers to interpret

Create Compelling Characters

Readers connect with characters who feel real and multidimensional.

  • Give characters flaws and strengths
  • Develop backstories that influence their actions
  • Create distinct voices for each character
  • Show character growth throughout the story

Exercise: Write a scene where your character faces their greatest fear.

Build Tension & Pace

Master the art of pacing to keep readers engaged from beginning to end.

  • Vary sentence length to control rhythm
  • Use shorter sentences for action scenes
  • Build tension through unanswered questions
  • Create stakes that matter to your characters

Tip: End chapters with hooks that make readers continue.

Edit Ruthlessly

Great writing is rewriting. Learn to kill your darlings and polish your prose.

  • Take time between writing and editing
  • Read your work aloud to catch awkward phrasing
  • Remove unnecessary words and phrases
  • Check for consistency in character details

Process: Write freely first, then edit for clarity and impact.

Write Authentic Dialogue

Dialogue should sound natural while moving the story forward.

  • Read dialogue aloud to test its flow
  • Use contractions to sound more natural
  • Reveal character through speech patterns
  • Balance dialogue with action and description

Exercise: Eavesdrop on conversations to study natural speech patterns.

Overcome Writer's Block

Every writer faces creative blocks. Here's how to push through:

  • Change your environment or writing tool
  • Set small, achievable writing goals
  • Write badly - give yourself permission to create imperfect drafts
  • Take a walk or do something creative in a different medium

Remember: You can't edit a blank page.
